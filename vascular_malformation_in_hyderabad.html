Vascular Malformation Treatment in Hyderabad, India

At One Stop Vascular Solutions, we specialize in providing advanced and comprehensive treatment for vascular malformations, addressing conditions involving veins, lymph vessels, and arteries. Our expert team is equipped to diagnose and treat various types of vascular malformations, ensuring personalized care for each patient.

Understanding Vascular Malformations:

Vascular malformations are congenital issues affecting blood vessels and may manifest in veins, lymph vessels, or both arteries and veins. The types include venous malformation, lymphatic malformation, venolymphatic malformation, and arteriovenous malformation. These conditions may remain asymptomatic but can be triggered by factors like adolescence, surgery, trauma, or pregnancy.

Types of Vascular Malformations:
  • Capillary Vascular Malformation (Port-Wine Stains): Involves capillaries, leading to flat, reddish-purple birthmarks known as port-wine stains.
  • Venous Malformation (Veins): Exclusively affects veins, causing masses or lesions and symptoms such as pain and swelling.
  • Lymphatic Malformations (Lymph Vessels): Limited to lymph vessels, forming fluid-filled cysts in soft tissues like the face, neck, or axillary regions.
  • Arteriovenous Malformation (Arteries and Veins): Involves both arteries and veins, creating abnormal connections and potential complications in areas like the brain or spine.
  • Haemangiomas: Noncancerous growths of blood vessels, often appearing as birthmarks in infants.
Causes of Vascular Malformations:

While present at birth, vascular malformations become apparent at different ages. The causes are linked to abnormal growth during the development of veins, arteries, or lymph vessels. There's no specific cause, and malformations can grow over time, varying in severity.

Symptoms of Vascular Malformations:

Symptoms depend on the type and location of malformations:

  • Venous Malformation: Pain, lumps under the skin, bleeding, ulceration, disfigurement.
  • Arteriovenous Malformation: Pain, stress on the heart, and bleeding based on the location.
  • Lymphatic Malformation: Fluid-filled cysts, bleeding, infection, and erosion into other organs.
Diagnosis at One Stop Vascular Solutions:

Our diagnostic approach includes a thorough physical examination, medical history assessment, and imaging tests such as ultrasound, MRI, and angiography. This helps us determine the type and extent of vascular malformations.

Treatment Options:

Treatment plans are tailored to the specific condition, vessel type, associated syndromes, and overall health. Options include:

  • Embolization: Closing problematic blood vessels with particles that will block the blood flow into the lesion.
  • Sclerotherapy or alcohol ablation: Injecting a chemical to close affected vessels by causing scarring.
  • Laser Therapy: Burning the lesion with laser energy from blood vessels.
  • Surgery: Recommended in certain cases usually in combination with other treatments.
Conclusion:

While vascular malformations are congenital, symptoms may not appear until adulthood. One Stop Vascular Solutions offers advanced and personalized treatment plans to manage symptoms and prevent complications. While there's no permanent cure, our expert team ensures comprehensive care for individuals with vascular malformations.
